01 Brand Foundation

Who We Are

White Door is the elevated expression of our parent brand — conceived not to compete with it, but to exist in an entirely different register. Where Westside moves quickly, White Door moves with intention. Where Westside speaks to everyone, White Door opens only for those who notice the details.

The name itself is the philosophy: a white door is at once visible and discreet, familiar and exclusive. You must choose to open it.

Where our parent brand speaks to everyone, White Door opens only for those who notice the details.

02 Logo & Monogram System

The Mark

Two rings, interlocked. Each whole on its own. Stronger together at the intersection — neither one's territory, yet belonging to both. The geometry is precise: two interlocked rings, flanked by two brass points — held in deliberate tension.

This is not a symbol designed to be explained. It is a mark to be recognised — quietly, with certainty, by those who already understand what they are looking at.

03 Typography

The Voices of White Door

Two typefaces. No compromise. Freight Display Pro carries the brand's soul — literary, classical, unhurried. Neue Haas Grotesk carries its precision — clean, unadorned, certain.

Didot is available for accent use only: editorial headlines in editorial contexts. It never appears in product communications.

✓ Typography Rules
  • Always left-align body copy — never justify
  • Generous leading: minimum 1.6× for body, 1.1× for display
  • Sentence case for all headlines and product names
  • All-caps only for UI labels, captions, and micro-copy
× Typography Prohibitions
  • Never center-align body text in any context
  • Never use bold weight in body copy
  • Never mix Freight and Didot in the same layout
  • Never use any other typeface without CCO approval

07 Lifestyle Worlds

How White Door Lives

White Door does not sell categories. It offers entry points into distinct ways of living — each with its own rhythm, material language, and emotional register. Every product, wherever it sits, belongs to a world. These are those worlds.

The wardrobe is not built for occasions. It is built for the life that moves between them.

12 Brand Governance

Who Holds the Standard

The Chief Creative Officer (CCO) is the final authority on all matters of brand expression. All significant brand decisions — new campaigns, partnerships, retail formats — require CCO sign-off before execution.

The standard is non-negotiable. The process exists to protect it.

Violation Protocol

Critical
Participation in any public discount event, end-of-season sale, or Westside promotional pricing scheme. Unauthorised use of the monogram or brand name in third-party contexts. Co-branding with Westside in any consumer-facing format. Price reduction outside the Archive Edit protocol. Actions requiring immediate remediation and CCO escalation.
Serious
Use of forbidden vocabulary in published communications. Non-compliant photography (flash-lit, cluttered, text-overlaid). Incorrect typeface usage in produced materials. Departure from approved colour palette in product or environment. Requires formal review within 5 business days.
Minor
Spacing inconsistencies in internal documents. Caption length exceeding 40 words on social. Sub-optimal lighting in store without corrective action taken. Logged and addressed in the next quarterly brand review.

Pricing Principle

Core Commitment
White Door does not participate in public sales, markdown events, or Westside promotional periods.

Our pricing is our positioning. Public discounting — percentage-off signage, EOSS participation, flash sales — surrenders the brand story and signals desperation. This is prohibited without exception. The single structured alternative is the Archive Edit, defined below.

The Archive Edit
Quarterly Inventory Protocol

White Door launches a new collection every quarter. Previous season inventory that has not sold is not discounted publicly — it is presented privately as the Archive Edit. This is the only permitted mechanism for price adjustment and must follow the protocol below precisely.

Who
Existing Customers Only
Invitation communicated by direct WhatsApp or email to customers with a confirmed purchase history. Never announced publicly — no social media, no window signage, no press.
How
Full Brand Standards
Archive pieces are styled, tagged, and presented as a curated edit — not a clearance rack. A single price is shown with no "was / now" comparison. The word "sale" or "discount" is never used by staff or in any communication.
When
2 Weeks, 4× Per Year
Each Archive Edit runs for a maximum of 2 weeks, triggered by the arrival of the new quarterly collection. It concludes before new collection product is fully displayed. Total duration cannot exceed 8 weeks per calendar year.
✓ Archive Edit — Permitted
  • Price reductions of up to 30% from original retail
  • Communication framed as "Previous Season" or "Archive Collection"
  • In-store only, or direct private communication to purchase history
  • Single final price displayed — no "was / now" framing
  • All White Door presentation and spatial standards maintained in full
× Archive Edit — Prohibited
  • Reductions exceeding 30% — any deeper reduction must route to Westside mainline (unbranded) or to a charitable donation partner
  • Any public signage, window display, or social post referencing reduced prices
  • The words "sale", "discount", "off", or "savings" in any format
  • Percentage-off framing in any communication channel
  • Running Archive Edit concurrently with a new collection launch
Post-Archive
Any White Door inventory unsold after the Archive Edit window closes is transferred to Westside mainline floor without White Door branding, or directed to a designated charitable partner. Under no circumstances is it publicly discounted further under the White Door name. CCO approves all post-archive transfers.
